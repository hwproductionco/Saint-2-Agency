Online Enrollment

There are two ways to have your client's customers enroll online from their own website.

  • Option 1 is the simplest, and requires no coding. (It's just a link.)
  • Option 2 allows far more customization and for the signup process to happen completely inside your client's website.

Option 1

A simpler and quicker option, for the technologically less inclined. However, this option currently does not allow the customization of the display with your client's branding elements.

This option will get your client's customer to a screen where they can enter their information. Once submitted, your client will receive an email with the customer's details, and the customer information will be automatically added to your client's account and campaign.

For this option, simply create a link on your site to the following URL:

http://www.clienttoolbox.com/signup.php?a=AAA&b=BBB&c=CCC&d=DDD

Make sure to replace the following placeholders:

  • AAA - This is the client's Account ID. You can find it near the bottom of the page when you click the "Edit" button next to the Account Information section in the Control Panel, when you log in to your client's account from in the listing of Clients when you click the "Clients" tab above. Example: "acmeinc".
  • BBB - This is your client's account security token (a long string of 32 characters). You can find it near the bottom of the page when you click the "Edit" button next to the Account Information section in the Control Panel, when you log in to your client's account from in the listing of Clients when you click the "Clients" tab above. Note: The Security Token will change if you change you client's account owner's password. Example: "89e495e7941cf9e40e6980d14a16bf023ccd4c91".
  • CCC - This is the ID of the campaign your client wants their customer to signup for. Campaign IDs are shown below the "Campaign Name" when you click the "Edit" button to the right of the campaign in the campaign list on the Control Panel screen.
  • DDD - The url you want your client's customer to go to after submitting their information. Make sure to type the full URL, including "http://" or "https://". Example: "http://www.yoursite.com/thank-you.html".

If you want to add a card number field, add "&card=" and provide your own label. NOTE: Replace spaces with the code "%20". For example:

http://www.clienttoolbox.com/signup.php?a=AAA&b=BBB&c=CCC&d=DDD&card=Membership%20ID

If you want to add a logo, add "&logo=" and provide your own URL. For example:

http://www.clienttoolbox.com/signup.php?a=AAA&b=BBB&c=CCC&d=DDD&logo=your_logo.png

If you want to add a notice above the instructions, add "¬ice=" and provide your own text. NOTE: Replace spaces with the code "%20". For example:

http://www.clienttoolbox.com/signup.php?a=AAA&b=BBB&c=CCC&d=DDD¬ice=This%20is%20a%20test%20notice.

If you want to use your own CSS, add "&css=" and provide your own URL. For example:

http://www.clienttoolbox.com/signup.php?a=AAA&b=BBB&c=CCC&d=DDD&css=http://www.clienttoolbox.com/display.css

You can combine all the fields in a (very long) URL.
You can also opt to send these fields as POST instead of GET, if you know what these mean (if not, don't worry about it -- It's for the geeks! ;-)

Option 2

Gives you much more control, and the ability to automatically add points, visits, dollars, etc. to a client's customer account for signing-up, but you or they need to be able to add or modify some files to their website and upload one to their website's server, or know someone who knows how to do that.

There are three steps to adding customer signup to a website:

  1. Make a page with the form for the customer to put in their information.
  2. Make a thank-you page to be shown to the customer once they have submitted their info.
  3. Upload the file that will process this form and send it to our server with the proper information.

Requirements

You, your client, or someone else must have the ability to add files to your client's web server (through FTP or some other method.)

The server where your client's site is hosted must has PHP enabled. (The vast majority of Web servers do, and it will be unusual for it not to.)

If neither requirements can be met, please use Option 1, above.

If your client is using a content management tool like Joomla, Mambo, or Drupal, include these steps as separate files inside a Wrapper or iFrame.

Step 2

Make a new page on your client's website to thank the user for signing up for the rewards program. Make a note of the URL of this page: we'll need to add it to the next step where we specify where to send the customer once they have submitted their info.

Step 3

Make a file called "register.php" (or any other filename as long as it ends with ".php" and matches the filename the form will be submitted to in STEP 1. This is a "bare" file, without any HTML - just the lines of code below.

In this file, copy-paste the following PHP code:

<?php$url = 'https://hwprod.clienttoolbox.com/admin.php'
$user_id = 'AAAAAAAA';
$user_token = 'BBBBBBBB';
$campaign_id = 'CCCCCCCC';
$redirect_url = 'DDDDDDDD';
//$auto_add = 'EEEEEEEE';
$ch = curl_init();
curl_setopt($ch, CURLOPT_URL, "$url");
$to_post = '&type=record_customer';
$to_post .= '&api=y';
$to_post .= '&a='.$user_id;
$to_post .= '&b='.$user_token;
$to_post .= '&c='.$campaign_id;
//$to_post .= '&card_number='.$_REQUEST['card_number'];

$to_post .= '&phone='.$_REQUEST['phone'];
$to_post .= '&street1='.$_REQUEST['street1'];
$to_post .= '&street2='.$_REQUEST['street2'];
$to_post .= '&city='.$_REQUEST['city'];
$to_post .= '&state='.$_REQUEST['state'];
$to_post .= '&postal_code='.$_REQUEST['postal_code'];
$to_post .= '&country='.$_REQUEST['country'];
$to_post .= '&custom1='.$_REQUEST['custom1'];
//$to_post .= '&auto_add='.$auto_add;
curl_setopt($ch, CURLOPT_POST, 1);
curl_setopt($ch, CURLOPT_POSTFIELDS, $to_post);
curl_setopt($ch, CURLOPT_SSL_VERIFYPEER,false);
curl_setopt($ch, CURLOPT_RETURNTRANSFER, TRUE);
$result = curl_exec($ch);
curl_close($ch);
header('Location: '.$redirect_url);
?>

Make sure to replace the following placeholders:

  • AAAAAAAA - This is the client's Account ID. You can find it near the bottom of the page when you click the "Edit" button next to the Account Information section in the Control Panel, when you log in to your client's account from in the listing of Clients when you click the "Clients" tab above. Example: "acmeinc".
  • BBBBBBBB - This is your client's account security token (a long string of 32 characters). You can find it near the bottom of the page when you click the "Edit" button next to the Account Information section in the Control Panel, when you log in to your client's account from in the listing of Clients when you click the "Clients" tab above. Note: The Security Token will change if you change you client's account owner's password. Example: "89e495e7941cf9e40e6980d14a16bf023ccd4c91".

The amount of points, visits, dollars, or name of product or service, depending on the campaign type, to automatically add to a customer's account in the campaign specified above. Once you have configured this file by replacing the placeholders above, upload the file to the client's site, to the same directory as the page that has the form from STEP 1.

Go ahead and test it out. After filling out the form, you should be redirected to the URL you specified, and within a few minutes, your client should get an email with the customer's details.

If your client wants to assign them a card number, (to be sent back to the customer via mail or email, for example) they just have to look-up the customer on the campaign the customer signed up for, "edit" the customer's info, and scan or enter in the card number for the card they want to assign to the customer, before they send it.
